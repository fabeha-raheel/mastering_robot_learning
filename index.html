04 — Must-Read Breakthrough Papers
NeurIPS 2019 · ETH Zurich
Learning Agile and Dynamic Motor Skills for Legged Robots (ANYmal)
The foundational paper showing RL can train quadruped locomotion from scratch in simulation and transfer to real hardware. Introduced the teacher-student training paradigm.
Science Robotics 2022 · ETH Zurich
Learning to Walk in Minutes Using Massively Parallel Deep RL
Demonstrates GPU-parallel RL training on hundreds of robots simultaneously. The paper that made locomotion learning practical and fast.
Science 2022 · ETH Zurich
Parkour with Legged Robots (ANYmal Extreme Parkour)
Shows robots learning to jump, climb, and vault over obstacles. Exemplifies exteroceptive + proprioceptive multi-modal policy learning for agile locomotion.
CoRL 2022 · Stanford / CMU
Diffusion Policy: Visuomotor Policy Learning via Action Diffusion
Introduced score-matching diffusion models for robot manipulation. Transformed imitation learning — now a core method in manipulation and loco-manipulation.
RSS 2023 · Stanford / CMU
Learning Fine-Grained Bimanual Manipulation with Low-Cost Hardware (ACT)
Action Chunking with Transformers — achieves human-level dexterity with imitation learning. Simple, powerful, and widely adopted.
arXiv 2022 · Google DeepMind
RT-1: Robotics Transformer for Real-World Control at Scale
First demonstration of training a single transformer-based robot policy on thousands of diverse real tasks. Established the "robotics foundation model" paradigm.
arXiv 2023 · Google DeepMind
RT-2: Vision-Language-Action Models Transfer Web Knowledge to Robotic Control
Showed VLMs (PaLM-E) can directly output robot actions. Bridges internet-scale knowledge with physical manipulation. Landmark for embodied AI.
ICRA 2024 · Berkeley + Stanford + Google
Open X-Embodiment: Robotic Learning Datasets and RT-X Models
Cross-embodiment dataset and model trained on 22 different robots. Demonstrates generalization across robot types — a new paradigm for embodied AI.
arXiv 2024 · Physical Intelligence
π0: A Vision-Language-Action Flow Model for General Robot Control
Most recent VLA breakthrough — flow matching + pre-trained VLM for general manipulation. Represents the current state of the art in robot learning.
ICRA 2024 · Berkeley / Stanford
OpenVLA: An Open-Source Vision-Language-Action Model
Fully open-source VLA — code, weights, and data. Best resource to practically study and fine-tune vision-language-action models for robotics.
NeurIPS 2020 · Berkeley
Dream to Control: Learning Behaviors by Latent Imagination (DreamerV1/V2)
The canonical world model paper. Model-based RL via learning a latent world model in a dream. Foundational for understanding imagination-based planning.
CoRL 2021 · ETH Zurich RSL
Rapid Motor Adaptation for Legged Robots (RMA)
Introduced online adaptation module for legged locomotion. Allows robots to adapt to new terrains in milliseconds. Hugely influential in field deployments.
05 — Study Strategy & Practical Tips
⚡
Active Coding from Day One
Don't just watch lectures — open a Jupyter notebook alongside every session. Implement algorithms like PPO, LQR, and trajectory optimization from scratch. The MIT and CMU courses provide Google Colab-ready notebooks you can run immediately.
📄
Paper Reading Protocol
Read papers in three passes: (1) title, abstract, figures only; (2) full read skipping proofs; (3) deep dive on methods. Always look up the paper's GitHub repo and run the code. Use arXiv's related papers feature to find follow-up work.
🔁
Simulation is Your Lab
Set up MuJoCo + Gymnasium in Week 1 and never stop using it. ETH's legged_gym in IsaacGym is free. You don't need real hardware — the best research groups train entirely in simulation and deploy zero-shot to real robots.
🏛️
Follow Research Groups Directly
Subscribe to ETH RSL (rsl.ethz.ch), MIT Robot Locomotion Group, CMU Robotics Institute, Berkeley BAIR Lab, and Stanford REAL Lab. They post preprints, code, and video results as they happen — your live curriculum for months 2-3.
🗓️
Daily 2-Hour Session Structure
First 60 mins: lecture video or paper reading with notes. Second 60 mins: hands-on coding, exercises, or implementing a concept from the reading. Never let a session be purely passive. End each week by summarizing 3 key ideas in writing.
🎯
Build a Personal Project
By Month 2, start a personal project: train a quadruped or biped in IsaacGym, implement a diffusion policy for a manipulation task, or fine-tune OpenVLA. A working demo is worth 10x the knowledge from passive reading alone.
